# Product

## Register

product

## Users

B2C job applicants who already have a usable CV and want to apply to roles faster without sounding generic or fake. The initial audience is tech and knowledge-worker candidates, including juniors, recent graduates, career switchers, and early-career professionals who need to tailor existing experience to each job posting.

They use the product while preparing applications: pasting a job description, reviewing the role's emphasized skills, deciding what they can honestly claim, generating a tailored CV, and creating a cover letter that uses their real context.

## Product Purpose

This product is a truth-constrained CV and cover-letter tailoring tool. It adapts a user's real background to a specific job without inventing seniority, ownership, impact, or expertise.

Success means a user can move from an existing CV and job description to an application package that is job-specific, ATS-readable, and recognizably theirs. The product should help users express familiarity, learning, and confidence at the right level instead of overstating their experience.

## Brand Personality

Calm, aesthetic, clean.

The voice should feel direct, useful, and grounded. It should help users make honest decisions about what to emphasize, what to soften, and what to leave out. It should not sound like generic AI career advice.

## Anti-references

Avoid clumsy, scattered, or visually noisy interfaces. Avoid generic "AI resume builder" positioning, exaggerated self-promotion, inflated cover-letter language, vague motivational copy, and output that makes juniors sound senior.

Do not make the product feel like a template marketplace, a gamified job board, or a marketing-heavy SaaS dashboard. The interface should not bury the user's real evidence under decorative UI.

## Design Principles

Truth before polish: every generated claim must trace back to the user's CV, profile, or explicit input.

Confidence has levels: distinguish expert, comfortable, familiar, learning, and "do not oversell" instead of treating every keyword match as expertise.

Application work should feel contained: guide users through one job-specific package at a time, with clear status and obvious next actions.

The user's voice is the source: require enough free-form career context to avoid generic AI cover letters and preserve the user's real motivation.

Show the reasoning: surface matched skills, gaps, warnings, and wording choices so users can trust and edit the result.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target standard WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard access, visible focus states, semantic form labels, and reduced-motion support. No additional accessibility requirements are currently specified.
